Misc. Notes
Matthew, son of Godfriedus Wing, or Wynge, lived in Banbury, Oxfordshire, Eng, from about 1560, and was a tailor by trade. His will was dated August 19, 1614, and proved Nov 15 following, showing that he died between those dates[NNY].

Will, proved 1614, 11, 15. Executors, sons Fulk and Thomas. He desires to be buried in the churchyard at Banbury. To the poor of Banbury, 10s. To eldest son Fulk, lease of house in which testator dwells and £20. To second son Thomas, £13. To third son John, 40s. To son-in-law Robert Chamberlain, £10. To daughter Johanna, 20s. To children of Fulk, viz., Anne, Dorcas, Mary and Matthew. To John, son of son Thomas. To Deborah and John, children of son John. To John Nichols, son of son-in-law John Nichols. To William Wing, son of fourth son James. To Thomas Chamberlain, son of son-in-law Robert Chamberlain. To children of Richard Gulliver,viz.,John, Thomas and Phebe. (Mr. Henry F. Waters' investigations brought to light this will and that of Matthew Wing's son John.)[OH]648, v45p236

Mathew lived not far from the Reindeer Inn, which was still in business in 1980.[Banbury Bapt. and Burials, 1558-1653, p. 198;Kathryn Grahm]209

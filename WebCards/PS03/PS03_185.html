Will of Margery Walles of Childerditch widow, 14 May, 33 Eliz. [1591]

To Mary Wadnall, 1 cow at 20 or marriage. To the poor people 40s. To Mary Wadnoll, 1 russet gown unlined and 1 red petticoat upperbodied with fustian. To Agnes wife of Richard Wadnall, 1 frize gown and 1 red petticoat. To Elizabeth wife of John Rainbird my russet gown. To Baker's wife of Childerditch my old russet gown. To Ellen Middleton widow 1 pair of sheets and 1 short tablecloth. To Gabriel Partridge my son William's son 2 kine and to Joan and Margery Partridge his daughters each 2 kine; he shall have the keeping of the six kine until they are 20. To John, Bartholomew, Gabriel, Katherine, Mary and Parnell Partridge 12 sheep and 8 lambs. I will that Bartholomew shall have the keeping of the sheep and lambs for their most advantage until they are 20. To Margery Partridge my son William's daughter 1 sow and 4 pigs at 20. To Gabriel, Joan and Margery each L6, 13s.4d., and he shall have the use of the L20 until they are 20. To Katherine, Mary and Parnell, my son Bartholomew's children, each L3 6s.8d. likewise. The residue of my goods to Bartholomew and William, whom I nominate exors. Wit. Niicholas Nevell writer hereof, George Raynbird, Admon. 13 Sep. 1591 on the oath of the exors.
